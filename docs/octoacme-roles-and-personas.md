# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for end-user experience and accessibility. They translate requirements into wireframes, prototypes, and design assets that guide development.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research, usability testing, and iterative design
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to align on features and interaction patterns
- Ensure deliverables are accessible, intuitive, and on-brand
- Maintain a shared design system or component library

### Goals
- Create experiences that are easy to use and meet accessibility standards
- Reduce rework by involving design early in the feature lifecycle
- Enable developers to implement designs accurately and efficiently

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- Works with **Product Managers** to clarify requirements, user stories, and priorities
- Partners with **Developers** to review implementations and validate designs in staging
- Engages with **Customer Support Representatives** to learn about usability pain points reported by users
- Provides design specs and prototypes for handoff via a shared design tool

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts provide data insights to support decision-making and guide outcome measurement strategies.

### Responsibilities
- Define tracking and analytics requirements with Product Managers and Developers
- Analyze usage, feature adoption, and performance data post-release
- Surface actionable metrics for retrospectives and planning
- Build and maintain dashboards for key project signals

### Goals
- Ensure every feature has clear, measurable success criteria
- Enable data-driven prioritization and retrospective discussions
- Identify trends and anomalies that surface opportunities or risks

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- Partners with **Product Managers** for success metrics definition and roadmap prioritization
- Works with **Developers** to define and implement instrumentation and data pipelines
- Shares findings with **Project Managers** during weekly syncs and retrospectives
- Provides post-release analysis to inform the next planning cycle

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain process, product, and support documentation to keep institutional knowledge accurate and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Document features, APIs, and internal processes clearly and consistently
- Convert subject-matter expertise into accessible guides for developers, support, and end users
- Review and update docs as part of the definition of done for each release
- Collaborate on FAQs and known-issues documentation

### Goals
- Reduce support burden by keeping documentation accurate and up-to-date
- Ensure new team members can onboard quickly using documentation
- Maintain a single source of truth for each process and feature

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- Works with **Developers** and **Product Managers** to verify accuracy and coverage of feature docs
- Collaborates with **Customer Support Representatives** on FAQs and known-issues content
- Reviews release notes drafted by the team and polishes them before publication
- Partners with **Project Managers** to keep process documentation aligned with current practices

---

## Customer Support Representative

### Role Summary
Customer Support Representatives act as the voice of the customer—triaging feedback and issues, escalating bugs, and contributing to documentation.

### Responsibilities
- Communicate user-reported issues and trends to engineering and Product Management
- Document support trends and suggest product improvements based on recurring themes
- Contribute to and review FAQ and support documentation
- Verify support readiness before each release

### Goals
- Reduce time-to-resolution for user issues
- Close the feedback loop between users and the product team
- Prevent known issues from shipping by contributing to pre-release checks

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- Collaborates with **Product Managers** to prioritize fixes for reported user issues
- Works with **Developers** for technical root-cause analysis and resolution guidance
- Partners with **Technical Writers** to keep support docs and FAQs current
- Provides pre-release readiness sign-off to ensure support tooling and documentation are ready

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
The Security Lead owns security practices, risk assessment, and incident response procedures across the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define and enforce standards for secure development and deployment
- Oversee risk analysis, threat modeling, and security scanning as part of CI/CD
- Lead incident response and post-incident review for security events
- Ensure compliance requirements are tracked and met

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Reduce mean time to detect and respond to security incidents
- Build a security-first culture across the engineering organization

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- Partners with **Developers** for secure coding practices, code review guidance, and CI security scans
- Works with **Product Managers** and **Project Managers** for risk management and compliance tracking
- Escalates critical security risks to project sponsors and relevant leads
- Coordinates with **Customer Support Representatives** on customer-facing communication during security incidents
